Lentil Dhal

Lentil Dhal is completely easy to prepare, even a short preparation process will convince you of this. In addition, it is not demanding on raw materials. The classic recipe uses coconut milk (from a can, not a carton) to soften the flavors, but as a last resort, you can also use cooking cream. As a side dish, I prefer couscous and whole wheat pita.

35 minutes
Servings
4
Ingredients
  • 300 g of raw red lentils
  • 400 g of chopped tomatoes
  • 600 ml of vegetable broth
  • 200 ml cooking cream or coconut milk (in a can)
  • 1 onion
  • 1 soup spoon of coconut oil
  • 1-2 soup spoon of garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of kurkuma
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 lemon juice
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • fresh coriander
Instructions
  1. In a pot or deep frying pan, fry finely chopped onions in hot oil.
  2. Then add raw lentils, tomatoes, broth, cream or coconut milk to it and mix well.
  3. Mix in the pepper and salt, cover and simmer for 25 minutes until thickened.
  4. Finally, add lemon juice, pressed cloves of garlic and fresh parsley or coriander to the lentils.
